## About the Workshop

This workshop introduces the main stages of academic research and publication. It guides participants from defining a research problem to preparing a paper for submission to a Q1 journal or a top-tier conference.

The workshop combines theoretical explanations, practical examples, structured templates, and hands-on activities.

---

## Topics

- Defining a clear research problem
- Identifying research gaps
- Writing research questions, aims, and objectives
- Conducting a literature review
- Critically analyzing research papers
- Reproducing baseline methods
- Improving existing methods
- Designing reliable experiments
- Evaluating and interpreting results
- Writing an academic paper
- Selecting suitable journals and conferences
- Responding to reviewer comments

## Workshop Structure

### Day 1 — Research Foundations

- Understanding the research process
- Defining a research problem
- Identifying a research gap
- Writing research questions and objectives
- Conducting and organizing a literature review
- Critically reviewing academic papers

### Day 2 — Methods and Experiments

- Understanding model architecture and methodology
- Reproducing baseline methods
- Designing controlled experiments
- Selecting datasets, metrics, and baselines
- Performing ablation studies
- Analyzing model strengths and limitations

### Day 3 — Writing and Publication

- Structuring an academic paper
- Presenting methods and results clearly
- Writing the introduction, methodology, results, and discussion
- Selecting suitable publication venues
- Preparing a strong submission
- Responding to reviewer comments

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of the workshop, participants should be able to:

- Formulate a focused and researchable problem
- Identify meaningful gaps in existing literature
- Develop clear research aims and objectives
- Organize and critically analyze related work
- Reproduce and evaluate baseline methods
- Design justified improvements
- Conduct reliable experiments
- Interpret and present results
- Structure and write an academic paper
- Select an appropriate journal or conference
- Prepare a professional response to reviewers
