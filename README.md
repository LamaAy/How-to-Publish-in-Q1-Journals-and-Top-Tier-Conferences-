## Overview

This repository contains training materials, templates, and practical guidance for students and early-career researchers who want to conduct high-quality research and publish in reputable journals and conferences.

The workshop follows the complete research journey:

**Research problem → Literature review → Research gap → Baseline reproduction → Proposed solution → Experimental design → Paper writing → Submission and revision**

## Workshop Goals

By the end of the workshop, participants should be able to:

- Define a clear and researchable problem.
- Formulate focused research questions, aims, and objectives.
- Conduct and critically analyze a literature review.
- Identify a meaningful and defensible research gap.
- Select, reproduce, and analyze appropriate baseline methods.
- Design fair, rigorous, and reproducible experiments.
- Explain novelty, contribution, significance, and impact.
- Write the main sections of an academic paper.
- Select suitable Q1 journals and top-tier conferences.
- Respond professionally and systematically to reviewer comments.

## Core Research Framework

| Stage | Guiding Question |
|---|---|
| **Background and Significance** | Why is the problem important? |
| **Technical Problem** | What is wrong with current methods or systems? |
| **Sub-Problems** | Which specific limitations create the main problem? |
| **Research Gap** | What has not yet been adequately addressed? |
| **Research Question** | What exactly will the study investigate? |
| **Research Aim** | What is the overall purpose of the study? |
| **Research Objectives** | Which measurable steps will achieve the aim? |
| **Proposed Solution** | How will the study address the gap? |
| **Novelty** | What is new compared with previous work? |
| **Contribution** | What will the study add to the field? |
| **Validation** | How will the proposed solution be evaluated? |

## Experimental Design Checklist

A strong experimental design should define:

- Datasets and data sources
- Data cleaning, preprocessing, and splitting
- Baseline and state-of-the-art methods
- Proposed architecture or framework
- Evaluation metrics
- Training settings and hardware
- Ablation studies
- Robustness testing
- Error analysis
- Statistical validation
- Computational cost
- Reproducibility details

## Academic Writing Principles

Effective academic writing should be:

- Clear, precise, and concise
- Evidence-based
- Logically organized
- Properly cited and referenced
- Objective in tone
- Free from unnecessary repetition
- Consistent in terminology and tense

A strong academic paragraph follows this structure:

> **Main idea → Evidence → Analysis → Conclusion or transition**

## Publication Strategy

A competitive submission should demonstrate:

1. An important and clearly defined research problem
2. A well-supported research gap
3. A meaningful and original contribution
4. Strong and fair experimental evaluation
5. Clear comparison with relevant baselines
6. Honest discussion of limitations
7. Appropriate venue selection
8. Precise academic writing
9. Full compliance with submission guidelines
10. A professional response to reviewer feedback

## Responding to Reviewers

Use the following response structure:

> **Thank → Acknowledge → Answer → Revise or clarify → Identify the change**

Reviewer comments should be treated as critiques of the paper, not of the researcher. Every response should remain objective, respectful, and supported by evidence.
